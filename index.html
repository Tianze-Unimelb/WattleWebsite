Why this matters

The problem

International students arrive caring about sustainability but learning a new city all at once — unfamiliar supermarkets, share-house kitchens, Myki, bin systems, budgets, language. Sustainability is rarely the first thing that fits.

The bet

Information helps. Belonging helps more. Students don't need another standard to fail — they need peers who've been a few months ahead, and habits that fit a new life.

Three everyday entry points

Small choices, peer-led, tested where students actually live and shop.

Chapter one

Food waste

The bulk-pack problem: shopping for one in a system built for families.

  • Two-meals-from-one-shop planning
  • Share-house fridge etiquette & "use first" zones
  • End-of-day market timing (Queen Vic, South Melbourne)
  • Repurposing leftovers across cuisines

Chapter two

Transport

When the cheapest, lowest-carbon option is also the most confusing one.

  • Myki confidence beyond the first tap-on
  • Reading PTV routes without anxiety
  • Walking & cycling: safe routes near campus
  • When ride-share is the wrong reflex

Chapter three

Consumption

First-semester buying is where most regret — and most waste — lives.

  • Op-shops & second-hand near student hubs
  • Borrow-don't-buy for one-semester needs
  • The "wait two weeks" rule for non-essentials
  • Buy Nothing & share groups by suburb

What I'd actually build

A first test, not a finished thing. Built to be edited after students see it.

Example resource

A 5-step food waste starter

  1. 01Plan two flexible meals before you shop.
  2. 02Buy ingredients that cross at least two dishes.
  3. 03Split bulk items with housemates on day one.
  4. 04Keep a "use first" shelf at the front of the fridge.
  5. 05Turn the week's leftovers into one wildcard meal.

How I'd test it through Wattle

Step 01

Listen

Sit down with 8–10 international students. Find the real barriers, not the ones I assume.

Step 02

Prototype

One short digital guide and one workshop deck. Built to be edited, not defended.

Step 03

Pilot

Run one workshop. Watch where people lean in, where they check their phones.

Step 04

Improve

Cut what didn't land. Find one student who'd co-lead the next session.

Success looks like

A student leaves a workshop with one habit they actually try that week — and a face they can text when they get stuck.
